CalSEVA Operational Guidelines & Best Practices

To ensure maximum accuracy, data integrity, and compliance while managing your industrial assets, all operators and technicians must adhere to the following guidelines when using CalSEVA.

1. System Setup & Data Entry

  • Standardized Asset Naming: Always use a consistent naming convention when registering new machinery (e.g., DEPT-TYPE-NUM -> QC-PRES-001). This ensures clean tracking and error-free search results.
  • Double-Check Master Tolerances: When entering Maximum Permissible Errors (MPE) or engineering formulas, verify the parameters directly against the machine manufacturer's manual before saving.
  • Input Validation: Ensure all raw values are entered with correct units (bar, psi, °C, mm). Garbage data in will result in inaccurate PDF reports.

2. Local Storage Management (Critical)

Because CalSEVA stores all data locally on your device for privacy and offline speed, users must manage their device storage responsibly:

  • Manual Backups: At the end of every shift or weekly cycle, export your generated PDF reports and device data to an external drive or secure corporate network. If the device is lost, damaged, or the app cache is cleared, un-exported data cannot be recovered.
  • Storage Allocation: Ensure your device has at least 200 MB of free internal storage to allow the seamless generation of PDF reports and database scaling.
  • App Updates: Before updating the CalSEVA app to a new version, perform a full export of your asset list to prevent accidental data overwrites during the installation process.

3. Report Generation & Sign-Offs

  • Review Raw Data: Before hitting "Generate PDF Report," use the report preview screen to verify that the "As-Found" (initial) data and calculated Mean/Error parameters are accurate and aligned.
  • Tamper Prevention: Treat generated PDFs as official documentation. Once generated and signed, do not modify the files externally. If an error is found, update the data within CalSEVA and regenerate a fresh report with a revised version number (e.g., v1.1).
